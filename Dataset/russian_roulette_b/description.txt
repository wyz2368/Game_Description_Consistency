Two players alternate turns with a six-chamber revolver, with Player 1 acting first. 
On each turn, a player chooses either to quit, and live on for sure, or to pull the trigger, and potentially kill himself.
Each trigger pull and its outcome are common knowledge before the next move. 
The chambers are numbered 1 through 6 according to the order in which they would be fired if players keep pulling the trigger.

If the player quits, he withdraws and the other player wins.
If he pulls the trigger, chance determines whether the gun fires; the firing probability equals one divided by the number of chambers that have not yet been pulled.
If he pulls the trigger and the current chamber is the loaded one, he shoots himself and the other player wins; otherwise, play passes to the other player.

If shooting himself, a player gets -1. 
If quitting, a player gets 0. 
If winning, a player gets 1.
