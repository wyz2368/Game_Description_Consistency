This is a zero-sum two-player game between the player and the adversarial host.
There are three closed doors: behind one is a car, and behind each of the other two is a goat.

The host first decides which door to hide the car behind, knowing that the player will try to find it.
Without observing the host's choice, the player then chooses one of the three doors.

The host observes the player's choice and decides whether to keep all remaining doors closed or to reveal a goat behind one of the unchosen doors.
When both unchosen doors hide goats, the host chooses which of the two to reveal.
If the host reveals a goat, the player observes which door was revealed and then chooses to either stick with their original door or switch to the remaining unopened door.
If the host keeps the doors closed, the player's original door stands as their final choice.

The player's final door is then opened: the player receives 1 if it hides the car and -1 if it hides a goat; the host's payoffs are the opposite of the player's.
