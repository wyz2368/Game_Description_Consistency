Consider a multi-stage game played between Country A and Country B.

Country A moves first, choosing whether to issue a threat or to ignore the provocation.
If Country A ignores, the game ends.
If Country A issues a threat, Country B observes this and chooses whether to back down or to issue a counter-threat.
If Country B backs down, the game ends.
If Country B counter-threatens, the game enters a crisis stage in which both countries simultaneously choose to retreat or to detonate, ending the game.

The terminal outcomes of the game are:
- "ignore": Country A ignores the provocation;
- "B backs down": Country A threatens and Country B backs down;
- "A wins crisis": in the crisis, Country A detonates and Country B retreats;
- "B wins crisis": in the crisis, Country B detonates and Country A retreats;
- "mutual retreat": in the crisis, both countries retreat;
- "mutual detonation": in the crisis, both countries detonate.

Country A's preferences, from most to least preferred, are:
"A wins crisis", "B backs down", "ignore", "mutual retreat", "B wins crisis", "mutual detonation".

Country B's preferences, from most to least preferred, are:
"B wins crisis", "ignore", then "B backs down" and "mutual retreat" tied, then "A wins crisis", then "mutual detonation".

Both countries receive a payoff of -100 at "mutual detonation", and Country A receives a payoff of 10 at "A wins crisis".
