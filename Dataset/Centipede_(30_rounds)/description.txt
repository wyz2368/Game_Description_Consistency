Consider two players, Alice and Bob, seated across a table from each other. Alice moves first.

At the start of the game, two piles of coins sit on Alice's side of the table: one pile contains 4 coins and the other contains 1 coin.

On each turn, the active player, the player on whose side the piles currently sit, chooses between two actions.
They may "take", keeping the larger pile and giving the smaller pile to the other player, in which case the game ends immediately;
or they may "push", sliding both piles across the table to the other player, who then becomes the active player.
Each push doubles both piles. Each player observes all previous moves.

For instance, if Alice takes on her very first move she gets 4 coins and Bob gets 1.
If instead Alice pushes and Bob then takes, Bob gets 8 and Alice gets 2; one push later, if Alice takes, she gets 16 and Bob gets 4.

The game lasts 60 moves at most: Alice makes the odd-numbered moves (1, 3,..., 59) and Bob makes the even-numbered moves (2, 4,..., 60).
If no one ever takes, Alice obtains the larger pile and Bob gets the smaller one.
