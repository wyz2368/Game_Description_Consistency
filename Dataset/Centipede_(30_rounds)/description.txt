Consider two players, Alice and Bob, seated across a table from each other. Alice moves first.

At the start of the game, two piles of coins sit on Alice's side of the table: one pile contains 4 coins and the other contains 1 coin.

On each turn, the active player —- the player on whose side the piles currently sit -— chooses one of two actions:
- take: keep the larger pile and give the smaller pile to the other player. In this case, the game ends immediately.
- push: slide both piles across the table to the other player. The other player then becomes the active player.

Each push doubles both piles, so the amounts at stake grow over time. For instance:
- if Alice takes on her very first move, she gets 4 coins and Bob gets 1;
- if Bob takes on his first move (after Alice pushes once), he gets 8 and Alice gets 2;
- if Alice takes on her second move (after two pushes), she gets 16 and Bob gets 4; and so on.

The game lasts 60 moves at most: Alice makes the odd-numbered moves (1, 3, 5, …, 59) and Bob makes the even-numbered moves (2, 4, …, 60).
If no one ever takes, Alice obtains the larger pile and Bob gets the smaller one.
