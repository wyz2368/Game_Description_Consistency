This is a two-player card game between Player One and Player Two, using a deck of three cards: King, Queen, and Jack (King beats Queen beats Jack).

A single move of chance draws an ordered pair of cards uniformly at random and assigns them to Player One and Player Two respectively; the third card is set aside.
Each player observes their own card and all actions taken by either player, but neither observes their opponent's card nor the undealt third card.

Player One moves first and chooses either to check or to bet.
If Player One bets, Player Two then chooses either to fold or to call.
If Player One checks, Player Two chooses either to check or to bet; if Player Two bets, Player One then chooses either to fold or to call.

The game ends in one of three ways.
If both players check, the player with the higher-ranked card receives 1 and the other receives -1.
If one player bets and the other folds, the betting player receives 1 and the folding player receives -1.
If one player bets and the other calls, the player with the higher-ranked card receives 2 and the other receives -2.
