The Country A can choose to escalate (E) or end the game by ignoring the provocation (I). If Country A ignores the provocation, both countries receive a positive payoff.
If the game escalates, Country B faces a similar choice: to back down (B), but lose face with a  negative payoff while Country A gains a positive payoff, or escalate (E).
Escalation leads to nuclear crisis: a simultaneous move game where each nation chooses to either retreat (R) and lose credibility or detonate. 
Unless both countries retreat, retaliation to the first nuclear strike culminates in nuclear disaster.
If one country detonates while the other retreats, the detonating country gains a positive payoff, while the retreating country incurs a negative payoff. However, if neither country retreats, retaliation follows the first nuclear strike, resulting in a catastrophic nuclear disaster, yielding a negative payoff for both countries. If both countries retreat, they each suffer a negative payoff.
