This is a two-player card game between Player One and Player Two, using a deck of three cards: King, Queen, and Jack (King beats Queen beats Jack).

Chance deals the cards in two stages: first, Player One is dealt a card uniformly at random from the three cards; then, after Player One's first decision, Player Two is dealt a card uniformly at random from the remaining two.
Each player observes their own card and all actions taken by either player, but neither observes their opponent's card nor the undealt third card.

Player One moves first and chooses either to check or to bet.
If Player One bets, Player Two then chooses either to fold or to call.
If Player One checks, Player Two chooses either to check or to bet; if Player Two bets, Player One then chooses either to fold or to call.

The game ends in one of three ways.
If both players check, the player with the higher-ranked card wins.
If one player bets and the other folds, the betting player wins.
If one player bets and the other calls, the player with the higher-ranked card wins.
A player who wins after both players check gets 1; the loser gets -1.
A player whose opponent folds gets 1; the folding player gets -1.
A player who wins after a bet is called gets 2; the loser gets -2.
