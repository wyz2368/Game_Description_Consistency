Player 1 and Player 2 vote on three candidates, A, B, and C. 
Each player's ballot is a strict ordering of the three candidates. 
Player 2 votes first; Player 1 votes afterward without knowing Player 2's choice.

Ballots are scored by Borda count:
- a candidate receives two points each time it is ranked first on a ballot,
- one point each time it is ranked second, and
- zero points each time it is ranked third.

The candidate with the highest total score wins.
If two or more candidates are tied for the highest score, the tie is broken according to the priority order A, B, C.
Among the tied candidates, the one appearing earliest in this order wins.

Each player's payoff depends only on which candidate wins, not on which ballots were submitted to produce that win.
Player 1 strictly prefers A winning to B winning, and B winning to C winning.
Player 2 strictly prefers C winning to B winning, and B winning to A winning.
