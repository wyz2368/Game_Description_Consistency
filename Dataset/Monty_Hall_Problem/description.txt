This is a single-player decision problem. There are three closed doors, with a car behind one door and a goat behind each of the other two.

Before any decisions are made, nature chooses uniformly at random which door the car is behind.
The player does not observe nature's choice; the host, who is not a player, knows.

The player first chooses one of the three doors. 
Then one of the two unchosen doors opens automatically, always revealing a goat; if both unchosen doors hide goats, either one may be opened; the choice carries no information relevant to the game and need not be modelled.
The player observes which door the host opened and then chooses either to stick with their original door or to switch to the remaining unopened door. 

The player gets 1 if the car is chosen.
The player gets 0 if a goat is chosen.
