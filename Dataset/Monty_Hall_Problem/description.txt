This is a single-player decision problem.
There are three closed doors, numbered 1, 2, and 3: behind one is a car, and behind each of the other two is a goat.

Nature first chooses uniformly at random which door hides the car.
Without observing nature's choice, the player then chooses one of the three doors.

After the player's first choice, the lowest-numbered unchosen door that hides a goat is opened to reveal the goat.
The player observes which door was opened. So now there is one chosen door, one opened door, and a final remaining unopened door.
Then, remembering their own first choice, the player chooses between the originally chosen door and the remaining unopened door.

If the car is behind the player's final choice, the player receives 1; otherwise, the player receives 0.
