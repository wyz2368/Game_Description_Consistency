Two players, A and B, take turns choosing one of two actions X and Y over 6 moves. 
A acts in moves 1, 3, 5 and B acts in moves 2, 4, 6. 
A public clock reveals the current round number to the moving player (equivalently, each player knows whether this is their 1st, 2nd, or 3rd personal move).
Players do not see the other player's actions and only remember their own last 1 actions. 
At the end of the game, each pair of moves (one from A and one from B) is compared: if both chose the same action, they each get 1 point; otherwise, they get 0. 
The final payoff is the total points each player earns, so the maximum possible payoff for each player is 3.
