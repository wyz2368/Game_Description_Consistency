Consider a game played between two players, A and B, over three rounds of two stages each.
Player A acts at stages 1, 3, and 5; Player B acts at stages 2, 4, and 6.
At every stage, the acting player selects an action from the set {X, Y}.

Players know the current stage number, but they do not observe their opponent's actions.
Moreover, the players have bounded memory: they recall only their own last action, forgetting any moves they made before that.

At the end of the third round, payoffs are determined by comparing the players' chosen actions within each respective round.
For any given round, if both players selected the same action, each receives a payoff of 1; if their actions differ, each receives 0.

A player's final payoff is the sum of the points earned across all three rounds, yielding a maximum possible payoff of 3 per player.
