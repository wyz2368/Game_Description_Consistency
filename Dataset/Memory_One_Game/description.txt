Consider a game played between two players, A and B, over six stages.
Player A acts at the odd-numbered stages and Player B at the even-numbered stages; at every stage, the acting player selects an action from the set {X, Y}.
The six stages are grouped into three rounds: round 1 comprises stages 1 and 2, round 2 stages 3 and 4, and round 3 stages 5 and 6.

Neither player observes the other's actions. The players also have imperfect recall; specifically,
when acting, a player's information consists only of the current stage number and their own most recent action (if any).

At the end of stage 6, payoffs are determined round by round.
In each round, if both players selected the same action that round, each receives 1 point for that round; if their actions differed, each receives 0.
A player's final payoff is the sum of points across the three rounds.
